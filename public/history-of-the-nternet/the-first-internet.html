The First Internet

ARPANET(The Advanced Research Projects Agency Network)

One of the world's first operational packet switching networks and is the first network to implement TCP/IP, and the origin of what was to become the global Internet.

1962

  • Scientist J.C.R. Licklider proposed a network of computers that are able to communicate with one another.

1969

  • First message sent by two computers from UCLA and Stanford University through ARPANET.

  • Ray Tomlin's development of the Email.
